Types of Orders

By Admin | Posted on 27 JULY 2024 | 07 min read

GTT Order

A GTT (Good Till Triggered) order is a type of order used in trading that allows investors to place a buy or sell order with a trigger condition. The order remains active until the specified trigger condition is met, after which it becomes a market order and is executed. This feature is particularly useful for automating trades based on specific price levels or conditions.

Key Characteristics
  • Trigger Condition

    • A GTT order is set with a trigger condition. This condition could be a specific price level at which the order will be activated.

  • Duration

    • The order remains active until the trigger condition is met or until it is manually canceled by the trader. Unlike standard limit or stop orders, which expire after a certain period, a GTT order does not expire until the trigger is activated.

  • Automated Execution

    • Once the trigger condition is met, the GTT order automatically becomes a market order (or limit order, depending on the type of GTT order placed) and is executed at the best available price.

Types of GTT Orders
  • GTT Buy Order

    • Definition: A GTT buy order is set to buy an asset only when its price reaches or falls below a specified trigger price.

    • Example: If you want to buy shares of Reliance Industries but only if the price drops to ₹2,400, you can set a GTT buy order with a trigger price of ₹2,400. The order will only be executed when the price reaches ₹2,400 or below.

  • GTT Sell Order

    • Definition: A GTT sell order is set to sell an asset only when its price reaches or rises above a specified trigger price.

    • Example: If you hold shares of Infosys and want to sell them if the price rises to ₹1,300, you can set a GTT sell order with a trigger price of ₹1,300. The order will be executed when the price reaches ₹1,300 or above.

Advantages of GTT Orders
  • Automated Trading

    • GTT orders automate the trading process based on predefined conditions, reducing the need for constant monitoring of the markets.

  • Precision in Execution

    • Provides a precise mechanism for entering or exiting positions based on specific price levels or market conditions.

  • No Expiry

    • Unlike regular orders that expire after a trading session or a specified period, GTT orders remain valid until triggered or manually canceled.

Disadvantages of GTT Orders
  • No Immediate Execution

    • The order will not execute until the trigger condition is met, which means there is no guarantee of immediate execution even after the trigger is activated.

  • Potential for Missed Opportunities

    • If the price moves quickly and does not reach the trigger level, the order might never be executed, leading to missed trading opportunities.

  • Manual Cancellation Required

    • Traders must manually cancel GTT orders if they no longer wish to execute the trade, which requires active management of the order status.

Example
Scenario

You want to buy shares of HDFC Bank but only if the price drops to ₹1,500. You place a GTT buy order with a trigger price of ₹1,500.

Execution

  • If HDFC Bank’s price falls to ₹1,500 or below, the GTT order is triggered and becomes a market order to buy the shares at the best available price.

  • If the price does not reach ₹1,500, the order remains inactive until the trigger condition is met or you manually cancel the order.

Comparison

  • Market Order: Executes immediately at the current market price but does not allow for specific trigger conditions.

  • Limit Order: Executes at a specified price but does not remain active indefinitely and may expire if not filled within a specified period.

  • GTT Order: Allows you to set a trigger condition for execution and remains active until the condition is met or manually cancelled.

Conclusion

A GTT (Good Till Triggered) order automates trade execution by remaining active until a specified trigger condition is met, offering precise control over entry and exit points. It provides a strategic advantage for automated trading but may miss opportunities if the trigger condition is never met or require manual cancellation.
