Types of Market

By Admin | Posted on 27 JULY 2024 | 07 min read

Markets include Primary Market, Secondary Market, Pre Market, Post Market

Pre Market

The pre-market session refers to the period before the official market opening during which trading activities can take place. This session allows investors to trade securities before the regular market hours, providing a glimpse into the market sentiment and potential price movements for the day.

Key Features of the Pre-Market Session

  1. Timing: The pre-market session in India typically occurs from 9:00 AM to 9:15 AM IST. This is divided into three distinct segments:

    1. Order Entry Session: From 9:00 AM to 9:08 AM, orders can be entered, modified, or canceled.

    2. Order Matching Session: From 9:08 AM to 9:12 AM, orders are matched and trades are confirmed, but no modifications or cancellations are allowed.

    3. Buffer Period: From 9:12 AM to 9:15 AM, the system processes the matched orders and prepares for the regular trading session.

  2. Purpose: The primary purpose of the pre-market session is to facilitate the discovery of the opening price for securities. It allows the market to absorb news and events that occurred overnight, helping to determine the initial trading levels.

  3. Order Types: During the pre-market session, investors can place limit orders, which specify a maximum or minimum price they are willing to buy or sell a security for. Market orders, which are executed at the current best available price, are typically not accepted during this period.

  4. Volatility: The pre-market session can be more volatile than regular trading hours due to lower liquidity and the processing of overnight news. This can result in significant price fluctuations based on limited trading volumes.

  5. Market Participants: Institutional investors, traders, and some individual investors participate in the pre-market session to take advantage of early trading opportunities or to react to news and events before the market officially opens.

Benefits of Pre-Market Trading

  1. Early Market Access: Investors can react to news, earnings reports, and other significant events that occurred outside regular trading hours.

  2. Price Discovery: Helps in determining the opening price for securities, providing a smoother transition into regular trading hours.

  3. Trading Opportunities: Offers additional trading opportunities for those looking to enter or exit positions based on overnight developments.

Risks of Pre-Market Trading

  1. Lower Liquidity: The pre-market session often has lower trading volumes, which can result in wider bid-ask spreads and less favorable prices.

  2. Higher Volatility: Price movements can be more pronounced due to the lower liquidity and fewer participants, leading to increased volatility.

  3. Limited Order Types: Certain order types, like market orders, may not be accepted, limiting the strategies investors can employ.

Example of Pre-Market Session

Let's say a company announced strong quarterly earnings after the market closed. In the pre-market session, investors react to this news by placing buy orders for the company's stock. As a result, the stock price may rise significantly before the regular market opens at 9:15 AM IST.

Conclusion

The pre-market session plays a crucial role in price discovery and provides investors with an opportunity to react to news and events before the official trading day begins. While it offers potential benefits, such as early access and additional trading opportunities, it also comes with risks like lower liquidity and higher volatility. Investors should weigh these factors and stay informed to make the most of pre-market trading.
